    Zendesk Support Standard Operating Procedure¶

    Objective: To establish a consistent approach for using Zendesk to manage customer support tickets, communicate with residents, and utilize reporting and analytics to improve service quality.

    Scope: This SOP applies to all resident experience support representatives, team leaders, and managers within the company who interact with residents and internal team members through Zendesk.

    Modify Date: May 29, 2024

    Responsibilities:

    Resident Experience Associates: Handle resident inquiries, adhere to the SOP, and maintain high-quality customer service.

    Team Leaders/Managers: Ensure the team is proficient in Zendesk, oversee resident communication, and analyze reports for service improvement.

    Procedures

    What is Zendesk?

    • A customer service platform designed to foster better customer relationships.
    • Provides tools for ticketing, support, customer communication, and more.

    Key Features of Zendesk:

    • Multi-channel Support: Handle customer inquiries via Email, Chat, and Phone.
    • Knowledge Base and Self-service: Empower customers to find answers independently.
    • Reporting and Analytics: Utilize dashboards for performance insights.
    • Integrations and Apps
    • Slack (Used for Security)
    • Zendesk Support App: Access Zendesk on the go for real-time support management.

    Setting Up Your Zendesk Account:

    • Log into Zendesk with your company-provided email address.
    • If you are unable to log-in, please reset your password by clicking on the link sent to your email and follow the prompt.

    Dashboard Familiarization:

    • Navigate through the Zendesk dashboard to understand the layout and functionalities.
    • Views (): This will be according to the location(s) that you support. You will only see your assigned tickets and those requiring attention for that location.
      • Unassigned
      • Recently Solved Tickets
      • Your unsolved tickets
      • Rated tickets from the past 7 days
    • Users/Customer (the Resident) ():
      • When a Zendesk Support request first arrives, it becomes a ticket and is automatically set to New.
      • When an agent is assigned to the ticket, the ticket is automatically set to Open.
      • If the agent has a follow-up question for the customer, the agent will set the ticket to Pending, which means that the agent is waiting for more information from the customer.
      • When the agent resolves the issue, they will set the ticket to Solved.
      • Finally, after a certain number of days, the ticket is automatically Closed and archived for later reference.

    Status:

    • New: Ticket created by a resident or team member.
    • Open: Resident replied to the initial email or reopened a solved ticket.
    • Pending: Waiting for a response from the resident.
    • Solved: Issue fully resolved or no response from the resident within 4 days.

    Understanding the Ticketing System/Ticket Management:

    • What is a ticket and how is it created?
    • There are several ways that a ticket can be created:
      • Email sent to support email for a specific location.
      • Direct ticket creation within Zendesk by a member of our team.
      • Email is sent to alias email or team members work email then forwarded over to the support email for the appropriate location
    • What is included in a ticket?

    • Public reply: Visible to everyone, including the requester. The ticket also includes a CC field, you can use to copy other external users (ex. roommates) on your reply.
    • Internal Reply: Private comments for internal communication only. For example, an agent may need to get advice from another agent to solve the requester's support issue. If you want to add an internal note, click the dropdown menu and choose Internal.
    • Requester: Person requesting support.
    • Assignee: Agent assigned to the ticket
    • Submitter: Agent opening a ticket for a resident.
    • Followers: Internal users receiving ticket updates.
    • Ways that residents or vendors can contact us before a ticket is created:
      • Send an email
      • Call you on the telephone
      • Text
      • Chat
    • Use views to prioritize tickets based on urgency and status.
    • Apply tags and macros for consistent categorization and responses.

    Ticket Types

    • Question: Requester has a question.
    • Problem: Issue affecting the requester and possibly others.
    • Incident: Used for occurrences of a problem that affects more than one person. Incident tickets are linked to a problem ticket and when the problem ticket is solved all the incidents of that problem are solved automatically at the same time.
    • Task: When you want to assign the ticket as a task to a specific agent. When you select a Task, you also set the Task Due Date.

    Ticket Status Types

    • Low: Tickets such as printing requests
    • Normal: Typical emails about parking, ticket dispute, general leasing inquiry, etc
    • High: Important request or incident but can slightly wait
    • Urgent: Emergency tickets such as maintenance requests, escalated resident situations, etc

    Responding to Tickets

    • Personalize communication: Use residents’ names and reference past interactions.
    • Company Tone and Language: Follow guidelines for clear, concise responses.
    • Ensure responses are clear, concise, and provide a resolution or next steps. Adding additional information as needed.
    • Use predefined macros for common responses to ensure consistency and efficiency depending on the topic that the requester is emailing about.

    Internal Team Communication

    • Utilize internal notes to communicate with team members within a ticket without notifying the resident.
    • Assign or reassign tickets to the appropriate team members when necessary.
    • Use @mentions to notify specific team members in ticket comments. This should be used especially when someone needs to take action, assist on a ticket or simply be informed.
    • Following tickets for updates

    Solving Tickets:

    • Personalize Communication: Reference past interactions and use the resident’s name.
    • Clear and Concise Responses: Provide resolutions or next steps clearly.
    • Follow the company's tone and language guidelines.
    • Predefined macros: Ensure consistency and efficiency for common responses.

    Reporting and Analytics

    • Regularly review Zendesk dashboard to track team performance, ticket trends, response times and resolution rates.
    • Customer satisfaction scores: Identify areas for improvement

    Best Practices for Support

    Managing Customer Interactions

    • Friendly Tone: Always maintain a friendly and approachable tone in your responses. This helps in building rapport and trust with the customer.
    • Empathy and Understanding: If you identify that the customer is facing a serious issue, shift your tone to be more understanding and empathetic. Acknowledge their concerns and assure them that you are there to help.
    • Clarity and Conciseness: Ensure your responses are clear and concise. Avoid using jargon and provide information in a straightforward manner.
    • Active Listening: Pay close attention to the customer's concerns and questions. Reflect back what you understand to ensure clarity.
    • Timeliness: Respond to customer inquiries as promptly as possible. Timely responses can significantly improve customer satisfaction.

    Email Management

    • Daily Check-ins: Check Zendesk emails at the beginning of the day to ensure no customer inquiries are left unattended.
    • Regular Monitoring: Continue to monitor Zendesk emails at regular intervals throughout the day to manage ongoing customer interactions effectively.

    Prioritizing and Handling Tickets Efficiently

    • Categorization: Categorize tickets to streamline the process of assigning and resolving them. Common categories include technical issues, billing inquiries, and general questions.
    • Time Management: Allocate specific time slots during the day to focus solely on ticket resolution. This helps in maintaining a steady workflow and prevents backlog.
    • Collaboration: Collaborate with team members to share the workload. Use internal communication tools to discuss and resolve complex issues.
